Genetic Diversity in Human Populations

Order:

Each row and colum represents a population. Each cell represents a comparison of two populations.

Cells with color indicate comparisons of populations in the same geographical area (see legend below), and gray cells indicate different areas.

Darker cells indicate higher genetic similarity (a lower fixation index).

Mouse over the cells to see each fixation index.

Use the menu above to reorder the cells by genetic similarity or by geography. Do you expect similar populations to be geographically close to one other?
